The Challenge

Before JamBox, this flagship retailer was flying blind during their most critical trading period.

Unpredictable Staffing

Managers scheduled staff based on gut feeling, often leaving the store overstaffed during quiet periods and understaffed during rushes.

Missed Conversion Opportunities

Without knowing how many people walked through the door, they couldn't calculate true conversion rates or identify improvement areas.

Holiday Planning Guesswork

Christmas staffing was based on last year's sales, not actual traffic patterns. They had no way to predict the pre-Christmas rush.

What JamBox Revealed

Evening Rush Discovery

Peak footfall consistently occurred between 6pm and 10pm, with 9-10pm being the busiest hour. This was surprising as the store had always focused staffing on lunch hours. The Times Square tourist crowd peaks in the evening.

Weekend Surge Pattern

Weekend traffic averaged 109,614 visitors daily compared to 88,889 on weekdays - a 23% increase. Saturdays and Sundays required fundamentally different staffing models.

Christmas Week Explosion

The week of December 20-28 saw daily footfall jump to 130,000-153,000 - nearly 60% above the monthly average. Dec 21st hit the peak at 153,406 visitors. Christmas Day itself saw 152,185 visitors.

Weather Impact Quantified

On December 3rd (13mm rain), footfall dropped 30% compared to similar dry days. This data now triggers automatic staffing adjustments when rain is forecast.

Actions Taken

Week 1

Shifted Evening Coverage

Moved two staff members from morning to evening shifts (5pm-close) based on hourly traffic data.

Week 2

Weekend Staffing Increase

Added 3 additional team members for Saturday and Sunday based on the 23% traffic uplift discovered.

Week 3

Christmas Week Preparation

Hired 5 seasonal staff specifically for Dec 20-28, informed by the footfall surge prediction.

Week 4

Weather-Based Scheduling

Integrated weather forecasts with staffing decisions - reducing coverage on forecast rainy days.
